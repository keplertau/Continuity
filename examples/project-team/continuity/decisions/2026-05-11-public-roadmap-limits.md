# Public Roadmap Limits

## The Decision

The public roadmap may name committed work and exploratory directions, but it must not publish dates unless an owner has accepted the date.

## What It Constrains

- Do not infer launch dates from internal optimism.
- Do not let session notes become public commitments.
- If a date is needed externally, record the owner and rationale in a decision entity first.

## Rationale

Dates without owners create false commitments. The project benefits from transparency, but not from promising more scheduling certainty than the team actually has.

## Evidence

- `sessions/2026-05-11-docs-navigation-review.md`

## Briefing Guidance

- Tier 1 inclusion: yes.
- Briefing form: public roadmap items cannot carry dates unless an owner has accepted the date.
- Retirement condition: if the project adopts a formal release-management process that supersedes this lightweight rule.
