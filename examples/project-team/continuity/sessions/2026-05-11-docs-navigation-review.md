## What Happened

The team compared two navigation models: role-based navigation and task-based navigation.

Role-based navigation looked tidy internally, but it required a new reader to know whether they were a user, developer, maintainer, or contributor before they could find the next page. Task-based navigation fit the reader's actual situation better.

The session also caught public-roadmap drift. A draft note implied dates that no one had accepted. The team locked the rule that public dates require an owner.
